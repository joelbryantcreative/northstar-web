Who Am I

Meaning over
everything.
Always.

I'm a documentary filmmaker and creative director based in Byron Bay. I've spent my career chasing the stories that actually matter. The ones that reveal something true about what it means to be alive, and our shared connection to each other and this earth.

I believe the best work comes from people who are fully themselves. That every frame is a choice. That craft without meaning is just noise. And that a camera, in the right hands, can bring more human connection into the world.

I picked up a camera at 14 and never put it down. Since then it's taken me across Australia, through Indonesia, Kenya, and beyond, documenting the people and stories that remind us what life is really about. My films have screened at international festivals. I've worked as Creative Director for some of the world's most-watched creators. And I've spent years building a creative life that funds itself... without compromising the work that matters.

"My camera saved my life and gave me purpose, now I use it to tell stories that matter."

What creative direction actually means

Visual identity

Defining the colour language, shot style, pacing, and aesthetic that makes a channel unmistakably itself.

Narrative strategy

Structuring content so it pulls people in, holds them, and leaves them wanting the next one. Documentary technique applied to YouTube.

Audience growth

Content that earns an audience through quality, not hacks. The kind of growth that compounds because people genuinely care.

Brand positioning

Helping creators understand what they stand for and how to express it — so their content attracts the right people at scale.

My journey

Built over years.
Not overnight.

I built a filmmaking career from scratch — without a safety net, without a famous mentor, without a film school network. I figured it out. Everything I teach comes from doing it in the real world, not theorising about it.

2014

A GoPro changes everything

At 14, a camera gave creativity a language. A trip through the Kimberleys opened the world. The connection between a lens and something real was made — and never lost.

2018

First paid work — real estate videos

At 17, the first money made with a camera. Proof that the craft could become a living. The connection between art and income clicked for the first time.

2020

YBS Youngbloods — Lead Videographer & Editor

At 20, moved to Western Australia to work inside one of YouTube's biggest ocean adventure channels with Brodie Moss. Helped grow Wade Pappenfus from 25K to over one million subscribers. Saw from the inside what content that actually scales looks like.

2024

The leap — Byron Bay

At 23, chose nature over security and went independent in Byron Bay. Built the brand from 9K to 40K through craft alone — a 30-day filmmaking challenge. No shortcuts. Just showing real work every day.

2025

Flowing Wood — Best Byron Bay Film, BBFF2025

At 24, Flowing Wood was selected for the Byron Bay Film Festival (winner), Noosa Film Festival, and France Anglet International Surf Film Festival. Three international selections. Peer-reviewed creative validation at the highest level the independent film world offers.

Now

Giving filmmakers the path I never had

Teaching the full system — craft, brand, income, and meaningful work — while continuing to tell the stories that matter. Everything built the hard way, now passed on so others don't have to figure it out alone.
